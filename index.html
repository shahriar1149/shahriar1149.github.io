About

Introduction

Engineering with curiosity

I completed my B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering with a major in Electronics and built a path across research, teaching, industrial exposure, communication, and student leadership.

Mindset

Learning in public

I enjoy explaining difficult concepts clearly, mentoring students, and combining technical depth with communication, organization, and practical action.

Direction

Impact-driven work

My strongest interests include metamaterials, THz waves, photonics, sensing devices, material science, and perovskite solar cells.

I come from a background in electrical engineering, but my journey has never been confined to a single discipline. From the beginning, I have been driven by a curiosity to understand how things work, not just in theory, but in real-world systems that shape how we live, build, and progress. This curiosity led me into the world of electronics, renewable energy, and advanced materials, where I began developing a strong foundation in both analytical thinking and practical problem-solving.

During my academic years, I focused deeply on electromagnetism, circuit design, and emerging technologies. My research work on terahertz metamaterial sensors allowed me to explore how engineered materials interact with electromagnetic waves, achieving high absorption and sensing efficiency. This experience sharpened my ability to work with simulation tools, analyze complex data, and think critically about how theoretical concepts translate into functional designs.

Beyond research, I sought hands-on experiences that challenged me to apply my knowledge in dynamic environments. I contributed to the electrical system design of a rocket project, where precision, teamwork, and adaptability were essential. At the same time, working as a teaching assistant allowed me to strengthen my communication skills and develop the ability to explain complex concepts in a clear and structured way.

Professionally, I have also explored the intersection of engineering and business through roles in technical sales and project support. These experiences helped me understand not only how systems are designed, but also how they are implemented, managed, and delivered in real-world scenarios. I learned to think from both a technical and practical perspective, balancing performance, efficiency, and user needs.

What defines me is not just my technical background, but my mindset. I am constantly looking for ways to improve systems, simplify processes, and create solutions that are both efficient and meaningful. I am particularly interested in sustainable technologies and energy-efficient systems, where engineering can have a direct and positive impact on the environment and society.

Today, I see myself at the intersection of engineering, innovation, and continuous learning. With the rapid advancement of AI and automation, I am actively exploring how intelligent systems can be used to build, optimize, and scale solutions with minimal barriers. I believe the future belongs to those who can combine technical knowledge with adaptability and vision, and I am committed to being part of that future.

This journey is still evolving. Every project, every challenge, and every new skill adds another layer to who I am becoming. I am not just building a career - I am building a path defined by curiosity, discipline, and the ambition to create meaningful impact.

Research

Where theory became design

Focus

Metamaterials and sensing

My undergraduate thesis focused on the design and analysis of a triple-band THz metamaterial with enhanced refractive index sensitivity.

Methods

Simulation and analysis

Used CST for simulation and OriginPro for analysis, optimization, and improved sensor performance evaluation.

Thesis

Enhanced refractive index sensitivity

The triple-band structure improved sensitivity by allowing more precise detection of refractive index changes.

Applications

Possible real-world uses

Materials testing, medical diagnostics, and chemical analysis all align with the kind of sensing capability explored in this work.

Supervision

Research guidance

The thesis was completed under the supervision of S.M. Anowarul Haque from April 2023 to June 2024.
