From Bangladesh to a Master’s Degree Abroad: The Complete Journey from Scratch to Success

Study Abroad • Sayek Shahriar

Teaching was not just something I did alongside engineering. It became one of the clearest ways I improved my communication, confidence, and understanding.

My experience in teaching started with tutoring, where I worked closely with students on physics, higher mathematics, and problem-solving. At first, I saw tutoring as a way to support students academically. Over time, I realized it was also helping me become a better engineer. Teaching forced me to organize ideas clearly, explain difficult concepts simply, and pay close attention to where people usually get confused.

When I later worked as a teaching assistant, that responsibility became broader and more structured. I was no longer only explaining a topic in one-on-one settings. I was also helping students in labs, assisting with assignments, preparing lecture materials, supporting question-and-answer sessions, and contributing to paper assessments. That environment taught me how educational support works at a larger scale and how important clear communication is inside technical learning spaces.

One of the most valuable things I learned is that understanding a topic for yourself is not the same as being able to explain it to someone else. Teaching exposed the gaps in my own understanding. When a student asked a question from a different angle, I had to rethink the concept, simplify it further, and make the logic more accessible. This process made my own knowledge stronger and more flexible.

Teaching also improved my patience and adaptability. Every student learns differently. Some need a conceptual overview first, while others need examples, repetition, or direct problem-solving practice. Learning how to respond to different needs helped me communicate more effectively, not only in academic settings but also in teamwork and professional contexts.

Looking back, tutoring and teaching assistantship shaped more than my communication style. They strengthened my discipline, empathy, and confidence. They also reinforced something important: technical knowledge becomes far more powerful when you can share it clearly with others.
